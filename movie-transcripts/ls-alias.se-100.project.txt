Aliases are a concise and powerful way to improve your productivity in the terminal.

Add the following aliases to your ~/.zshrc file:

alias l='ls -la'
alias ls='ls -l'

The a flag in the ls command is used to show hidden files and directories. The l flag is used to show the long format of the output.


SHOW: source ~/.zshrc
You will likely want to create the alias

alias zshrc='vim ~/.zshrc'
alias vimrc='vim ~/.vimrc'
alias tmuxconf='vim ~/.tmux.conf'
alias gitconfig='vim ~/.gitconfig'

To quickly edit your configuration files.

Aliases are fun and can be used in creative ways to make your terminal experience more enjoyable.
